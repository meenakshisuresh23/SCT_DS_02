Task: Data Visualization (COVID-19 India Analysis)

Project Overview
This project involved performing Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on the initial phase of the COVID-19 outbreak in India. The goal was to identify the correlation between health outcomes and track the progression of recoveries over time.

Tools Used
Power BI Desktop: Used for data modeling and advanced visualizations.
Excel/CSV: Source data handling.

Dataset Used
File Name: `Covid19 India (Jan 20 - Mar 20).csv`


Visualizations and Insights
1. Relationship Analysis: Cured vs. Deaths (Scatter Plot)
Visual: Scatter Plot grouped by State/Union Territory.
Pattern: Strong Positive Linear Correlation.
Insight: The data points are tightly packed along a diagonal path, suggesting that the ratio between recoveries and fatalities remained relatively constant across various states.
Anomaly Detection: A significant outlier was identified at the top right of the chart. This represents a major hotspot with a significantly higher volume of cases (both recoveries and deaths) compared to the rest of the country.
2. Trend Analysis: Count of Cured by Date (Line Chart)
Visual: Line Chart showing the temporal progression of the recovery phase.
Trend: Stagnation to Growth.
Insight: The visualization shows a long flat line throughout January and February (stagnation), where very few recoveries were recorded. 
Observation: A "staircase" increase begins in March, marking the point where the first major wave of patients reached the end of their infection cycle and medical interventions began showing measurable results.
